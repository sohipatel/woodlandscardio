Ultrasounds<\/h1>\n

Echocardiogram<\/h3>\n

An echocardiogram allows doctors to see the heart beating, and to see the heart valves and other structures of the heart.<\/p>\n

Occasionally, your lungs, ribs, or body tissue may prevent the sound waves and echoes from providing a clear picture of heart function. If so, the sonographer may inject a small amount of liquid (contrast) through an IV to better see the inside of the heart.<\/p>\n

Carotid Ultrasound<\/h3>\n

Carotid ultrasound is a painless and harmless test that uses high-frequency sound waves to create pictures of the insides of your carotid arteries.<\/p>\n

You have two common carotid arteries, one on each side of your neck. They each divide into internal and external carotid arteries.<\/p>\n

The internal carotid arteries supply oxygen-rich blood to your brain. The external carotid arteries supply oxygen-rich blood to your face, scalp, and neck.<\/p>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
